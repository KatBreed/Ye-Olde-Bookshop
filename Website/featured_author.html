Featured Author / Book of the Month

Ernest Hemingway

Early Life

Ernest Hemingway was born in Cicero (now in Oak Park), Illinois, USA on July 21 1899. The second of six children. His parents were Clarence Edmonds and Grace Hall, a doctor and musician respectively. An active person, he began to write in high school and treasured summers with his family. During World War 1 he served as an ambulance driver on the Italian Front in Milan until he was wounded. He was unable to join the U.S. Army due to poor eyesight.

Refocused Author

After returning home from the war wounded and readjusting, he refocused on his writing. Pretty soon he was writing stories for the Toronto Star. Hemingway eventually relocated to Paris, a hotbed of interesting people and artists. In 1954 he was awarded the Nobel Prize in Literature

Private Life and Death

Ernest Hemingway's private life included four marriages and three children. Plagued by pain and illness after two successive plane crashes in 1954. While also battling anxiety and despression, in 1961 Hemingway took his own life. A fate well known in his family.

The Old Man and the Sea

Synopsis

The Old Man and the Sea is a short novella that tells the story of Santiago, a cuban fisherman down on his luck for the last eighty-four days. Santiago decides to take a skiff into the deep Gulf Stream where he encounters a giant Marlin. After struggling with it for three days, he manages to tie it to his boat and make for home. However, sharks are attracted to the Marlin. Although Santiago kills a few, they leave behind only the skeleton.

Critical Reception

Upon the release of The Old Man and the Sea in 1952, Hemingway found himself an international celebrity and his reputation reinstated. In 1953 the novella won a Pulitzer Prize. He specifically cited The Old Man and the Sea when he won the Nobel Prize in 1954 and dedicated it to the Cuban people.
