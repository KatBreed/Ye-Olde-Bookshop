Featured Author

Ernest Millar Hemingway was born July 21, 1899. An American Nobel Prize winning author who wrote seven novels, six short-story collections, and two non-fiction works. His style was minimalistic. A technique Hemingway coined as the Iceberg Theory. His style was also masculine and he had a widely publicized life that included four marriages.

He was a reporter during college and served as an ambulance driver during World War One. A defective eye kept him from military service. Upon recuperating at home he regained his focus in writing. Ending up in Paris, he saw his first works published there. Including an important collection of stories called In Our Time.

Book of the Month

The Old Man and the Sea is a Pulitzer Prize winning novella written by Hemingway and first published in 1952. This last major work of his. Set in Havana, it tells the story of an aged Cuban fisherman suffering from an eighty-four day unlucky streak. On the eighty-fifth day he encounters a Marlin and battles to bring it back home.

The Old Man and the Sea reinstated Hemingway's place in the world of Literature as his previous work, a full novel Across the River and into the Trees, was not recieved by critics very well.
